The Hyde Park Relays Remembered

Hyde Park Relays Remembered [Jennie Cox]

A history of the event throughout its first 50 years. It was first printed in the 50th HPR programme to commemorate the long running of the event and this special date in its history. The information was compiled and the text written by Jennie Cox, who ran for Imperial College for many years and won the Hyde Park Relays several times.

Some facts about the Hyde Park Relays

Here are a few interesting facts about the relays which were turned up during research for 'Hyde Park Relays Remembered...' and the 50th running of the event:

  • The event used to be known as the ‘Hyde Park Road Relay.’
  • It was the idea of Tony Watts and Bob Giggs in January 1949
    (possibly the swiftest organisation in the history of the event).
  • Loughborough men have won the team event 14 times.
  • Brunel (WLIoHE) and Oxford University have both won the women’s relay 4 times.
  • D.M. Turner from Queen’s College, Cambridge, ran the fastest leg on 6 occasions.
  • 1988 saw the largest entry list, of over 1000 competitors.
